# 💡 Why This Project Exists

## The problem

Creative reviews are full of recommendations.

> "Make the face bigger."

> "Increase the contrast."

> "The composition feels cluttered."

While this feedback may improve an individual asset, it rarely explains **why** those changes matter.

Without a clear chain of reasoning, teams improve one piece of creative at a time but don't necessarily improve the way they think about creative decisions. Feedback becomes dependent on individual experience, personal preference, or instinct, making it difficult to teach, scale, or apply consistently.

As creative work becomes increasingly measurable, evaluated, and assisted by AI, the need for explainable creative reasoning becomes even more important.

This project began with a simple question:

> **Can creative judgment become more explainable?**

---

# 💭 The philosophy

Every creative decision influences how an audience thinks, feels, and acts.

Rather than asking whether a piece of creative is "good," Audience-First Creative Reasoning asks a different question:

> **How is this creative likely to influence audience behaviour, and what business outcome is that likely to create?**

Every recommendation should be supported by a clear chain of reasoning:

```text
Creative Observation
        ↓
Audience Psychology
        ↓
Business Outcome
        ↓
Recommendation
```

The objective is not simply to improve individual creative assets.

The objective is to improve the quality of creative decision-making.

---

# ⚙️ The methodology

To make creative judgment more transparent, the project is developing a methodology built around a sequence of **Reasoning Gates**.

Each gate answers one critical question before moving to the next.

Some gates act as prerequisites and therefore carry greater weight because they determine whether subsequent evaluation is meaningful.

Rather than functioning as a weighted checklist, the Reasoning Gates are designed to reflect the way audiences process visual information—from initial attention, through comprehension, to emotional engagement, and ultimately action.

---

# ⭐ Current development

The methodology is being developed iteratively, with each Reasoning Gate building on the principles established before it.

The first gate establishes the foundation for the reasoning model. Additional gates are currently being researched, tested, and refined before publication.

### Current roadmap

✅ **Gate 1 — Earn Qualified Attention** [LINK](https://github.com/jia-goh/Audience-First-Creative-Reasoning/blob/bd16416ca5f81319ebcee3b117473a093abdb090/gate1.md)

The first published gate explores how creative earns attention from its intended audience and introduces the concept of **Qualified Attention** as the foundation for all subsequent evaluation.

✅ **Gate 2 — Every Image Makes a Promise** [LINK](https://github.com/jia-goh/Audience-First-Creative-Reasoning/blob/bd16416ca5f81319ebcee3b117473a093abdb090/gate1.md)

This gate explores how audiences form expectations from visual cues within the first moments of viewing, and why aligning that promise with the underlying content is critical for building trust and sustaining engagement.

🚧 **Gate 3 – The Audience is Scanning, Not Studying** *(currently in development)*

This gate explores how audiences rapidly scan visual information before deciding whether to engage, and why reducing cognitive effort helps communicate the story more quickly and accurately.

🚧 **Additional Reasoning Gates** *(currently in development)*

Future gates will continue to build a sequential reasoning model that connects visual design, audience psychology, and business outcomes.

# 🔎 Current scope

Version 1 focuses exclusively on **static creative artwork**.

This includes applications such as:

* Streaming key art
* Movie posters
* Social media creatives
* Display advertising
* YouTube thumbnails
* App store artwork

By deliberately narrowing the scope, the project aims to develop a deeper understanding of creative reasoning before expanding into other creative formats.

---

# 🗓️ Long-term vision

The long-term ambition is to evolve Audience-First Creative Reasoning into an AI-assisted reasoning engine capable of evaluating static creative artwork using structured, explainable reasoning.

Rather than replacing creative judgment, the goal is to make it more transparent, teachable, and scalable.

By making the reasoning behind creative decisions explicit, the project aims to help marketers, creative strategists, and product teams make more consistent creative decisions while providing a stronger foundation for future AI-assisted creative evaluation.

---

# ✨ Building in the open

This repository documents the ongoing evolution of the methodology.

Ideas will change.

New research will challenge existing assumptions.

Some principles will be refined, while others may be replaced entirely.

That is intentional.

Creative reasoning is not a finished discipline, and this project does not claim to have all the answers.

Instead, the goal is to openly develop, test, and refine a more explainable way of evaluating creative effectiveness — one that others can critique, contribute to, and build upon over time.
