# Gate 1 — Earn Qualified Attention
## 🧱 Foundational Truth
> ### People notice before they engage.

## 🎯 Objective
The first responsibility of an image is to earn **Qualified Attention**.

Qualified Attention is the moment when the intended audience chooses to invest another second because the image signals an authentic and relevant story worth exploring.

The goal is not to maximise attention.

The goal is to earn the **_right attention_** from the **_right audience_** for the **_right reasons_**.

## 🤔 Why It Matters
Every image competes for attention in a fast-scrolling environment, whether that's a streaming app, social feed, or digital campaign.

If an image fails to earn attention, nothing else gets processed.

If it earns attention from the wrong audience, it may generate impressions or clicks, but those interactions rarely translate into meaningful engagement or business impact.

By earning Qualified Attention from the outset, creative works harder throughout the entire customer journey. It reduces wasted impressions, improves the quality of engagement, increases return on creative investment, and, in advertising, contributes to stronger return on ad spend (ROAS).

## 👉 Qualified Attention vs. Attention Debt
Not all attention is valuable.

**Qualified Attention** is earned because the audience recognises something authentic, relevant, or emotionally meaningful.

**Attention Debt** is created when attention is earned through misleading, sensational or superficial tactics that don't reflect the actual content.

Examples include:
- Clickbait imagery
- Cute or visually attractive elements unrelated to the story
- Shock value with no narrative purpose
- Visual gimmicks that distract from the core message
  
Attention Debt may increase initial clicks, but often results in lower engagement, weaker trust, and poorer long-term performance.

## 👤 How the Audience Thinks
People rarely stop because an image is louder or busier.

They stop because something makes them think:
> ### "This might be worth another second."
That reason may be:
- A compelling human expression
- An emotionally charged moment
- Something unexpected
- A familiar idea presented in a fresh way
- A visually striking composition
- A signal that the content is relevant to them
- Different audiences stop for different reasons.

The evaluator's role is to determine whether the intended audience is likely to invest another second.

## Gate Evaluation
### Gate Question
> ### Has this image earned Qualified Attention from its intended audience?

1️⃣ Step 1 — Gather Evidence

The evaluator should first collect objective evidence before drawing conclusions.

🪜 **Visual Hierarchy**

Observe:
- Relative size of the primary subject or object
- Number of competing focal points
- Contrast between the primary subject and background
- Separation of the primary subject from surrounding elements
- First point of visual attention

👥 **Audience Signals**

Observe:
- Recognisable genre cues
- Character expression clarity
- Clarity of genre setting through background and environment
- Overall tonal consistency
- Signals that indicate who this content is intended for

2️⃣ Step 2 — Reason

Using the evidence collected, determine:

- What is the audience most likely to notice first?
- Is that what we want them to notice first?
- Is the image likely to earn another second from the intended audience?
- Is attention being earned through the story, or through superficial attention-grabbing tactics?
- Does the image establish enough interest to continue evaluating the story promise?

3️⃣ Step 3 — Gate Decision

**PASS** ✅
The image earns Qualified Attention through authentic and relevant visual signals that encourage the intended audience to invest another second.

**FAIL** ❌
The image is ignored, misunderstood, or earns attention for reasons unrelated to the story or intended audience. If this gate is not passed, recommendations should focus on improving Qualified Attention before evaluating any subsequent gates.

## ✨ Recommendation Framework
Every recommendation should connect:

> ### Observation → Reasoning → Recommendation → Desired Audience Behaviour

Don't simply explain what should change.

Explain why it matters.

For example:

❌ Increase the protagonist's size.

✅ Increase the protagonist's visual prominence so viewers can identify the emotional anchor more quickly. Faster recognition reduces cognitive effort during the first second of viewing, increasing the likelihood that the intended audience invests another second and continues exploring the content.

## 🧠 Reviewer's Creed

Creative should not optimise for the most attention.

Creative should optimise for the most Qualified Attention.

Every image has one opportunity to earn another second.

Make it count.
